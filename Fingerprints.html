There is a concept of “CA”, which stands for Certificate Authority. Websites prove their identities to these CA’s through real world documentation. When the CA believes the website is authentic, they digitally sign the website’s security certificate, placing their reputation on the line and vouching that the site is authentic.

SSL interception is a very real threat that people are almost always in danger of. SSL interception cannot be prevented but it can be detected. When SSL interception is occurring, the public key will be different from the authentic public key.

When dealing in Cyber-security, there is a threat of false positives and false negatives. A false positive is when a positive is detected, however, in actuality, it is a negative. A false negative is when a negative is detected, however, in actuality, it is a positive. However, in Cyber Security, a false positive is when a threat is detected but there isn’t one, and a false negative is when no threat is detected but there is one.
